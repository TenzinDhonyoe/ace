# CHEM250 — synthetic lecture digest

## Lecture 1 — Functional groups (p. 1–12)
Alkane, alkene, alkyne, alcohol, ether, aldehyde, ketone, carboxylic acid,
ester, amide, amine, nitrile, halide. IUPAC naming priority: carboxylic
acid > ester > amide > nitrile > aldehyde > ketone > alcohol > amine.

## Lecture 2 — Substitution: SN1 vs SN2 (p. 13–28)
SN2 bimolecular, concerted, inversion (Walden inversion), favored by
primary carbons, strong nucleophile, polar aprotic solvent (DMSO, DMF,
acetone). SN1 unimolecular, stepwise via carbocation, racemization,
favored by tertiary carbons, weak nucleophile, polar protic solvent
(water, alcohols).

Rate: SN2 = k[R-X][Nu⁻], SN1 = k[R-X].

## Lecture 3 — Elimination: E1 vs E2 (p. 29–42)
E2 bimolecular, anti-periplanar, Zaitsev product (more substituted alkene)
favored unless bulky base (then Hofmann). E1 via carbocation, similar
conditions to SN1. E2 vs SN2 often compete — strong bulky base favors E2.

## Lecture 4 — Alkene addition (p. 43–60)
Markovnikov addition: H adds to less substituted carbon, X to more
substituted. Anti-Markovnikov via peroxides (HBr only). Hydroboration-
oxidation gives anti-Markov alcohol. Halogenation (Br₂) gives anti
vicinal dihalide via bromonium intermediate.
