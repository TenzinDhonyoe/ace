# HIST310 — synthetic lecture digest

## Lecture 1 — Origins of WWI (p. 1–22)
Triple Entente (France, Russia, Britain) vs Triple Alliance (Germany,
Austria-Hungary, Italy). July 28, 1914: Austria declares war on Serbia
after Franz Ferdinand assassination (June 28). Schlieffen Plan assumed
France falls in 6 weeks; failed at the Marne (Sept 1914). Historiographical
debate: Fischer thesis (1961) argues Germany bore primary responsibility,
Clark (2012, "Sleepwalkers") argues shared miscalculation.

## Lecture 2 — Interwar (p. 23–48)
Versailles (1919): Article 231 "war guilt," 132B gold marks reparations,
Rhineland demilitarized, army capped at 100k. Weimar hyperinflation 1923
(1 USD = 4.2T marks at peak). Dawes Plan 1924 stabilized currency.
Great Depression hit Germany hardest; Nazi vote share 2.6% (1928) →
37.3% (July 1932). Hitler appointed Chancellor Jan 30, 1933.

## Lecture 3 — WWII Europe (p. 49–74)
Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact Aug 1939. Invasion of Poland Sept 1, 1939.
Fall of France May-June 1940. Operation Barbarossa June 22, 1941
(largest invasion in history, 3.8M Axis troops). Stalingrad Aug 1942 –
Feb 1943, turning point on Eastern Front. D-Day June 6, 1944.
Holocaust: industrialized murder of ~6M Jews; Wannsee Conference Jan
1942 coordinated "Final Solution." Auschwitz liberated Jan 27, 1945.

## Lecture 4 — Cold War beginnings (p. 75–95)
Yalta Feb 1945 (FDR, Churchill, Stalin) — spheres of influence in
Europe. Truman Doctrine March 1947, Marshall Plan June 1947 ($13B
to Western Europe). Berlin Blockade June 1948 – May 1949 met with
airlift. NATO founded April 1949, Warsaw Pact 1955.
