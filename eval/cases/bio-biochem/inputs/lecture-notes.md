## Lecture 1 — Amino acids (p. 1–14)
20 proteinogenic amino acids. Side chain chemistry determines folding.
Non-polar (Gly, Ala, Val, Leu, Ile, Met, Phe, Trp, Pro), polar uncharged
(Ser, Thr, Cys, Tyr, Asn, Gln), positive (Lys, Arg, His), negative (Asp, Glu).
Peptide bond formation, Ramachandran plot, α-helix and β-sheet secondary
structure. Tertiary via disulfide bridges, salt bridges, hydrophobic core.

## Lecture 2 — Enzyme kinetics (p. 15–32)
Michaelis-Menten: v = V_max[S] / (K_m + [S]). K_m = substrate concentration
at half V_max. Lineweaver-Burk double-reciprocal linearization.
Competitive inhibition increases apparent K_m, V_max unchanged.
Non-competitive decreases V_max, K_m unchanged. Uncompetitive decreases
both proportionally.

## Lecture 3 — Glycolysis & TCA (p. 33–55)
Glucose → 2 pyruvate, net 2 ATP + 2 NADH. Committed step: PFK-1
(phosphofructokinase), allosterically inhibited by ATP and citrate,
activated by AMP and F2,6BP. TCA cycle: acetyl-CoA → 2 CO₂, net 3 NADH
+ 1 FADH₂ + 1 GTP per turn.

## Lecture 4 — Ox phos (p. 56–72)
Electron transport chain Complex I–IV. Proton gradient drives ATP synthase
(Complex V). P/O ratio ≈ 2.5 for NADH, 1.5 for FADH₂. Uncouplers
(DNP, thermogenin) dissipate the gradient as heat.
